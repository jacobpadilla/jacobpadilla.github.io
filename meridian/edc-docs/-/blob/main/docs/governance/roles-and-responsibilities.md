# Roles & Responsibilities

The Enterprise Data Catalog's governance model relies on clearly defined roles with distinct responsibilities. This page describes each role, who fills it, and what is expected of them.

## Role Summary

| Role | Scope | Filled By | Approximate Count |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Chief Data Officer (CDO) | Enterprise-wide | Executive appointment | 1 |
| Functional Domain Manager (FDM) | One Functional Domain | Senior manager or principal IC | 6 (one per domain) |
| System Owner | One source system within a domain | Technical lead or application manager | ~15 across all domains |
| Data Steward | One or more Data Assets | Analyst, engineer, or business SME | ~40 across all domains |
| Data Consumer | Read-only catalog access | Any Meridian employee | All employees |
| Catalog Platform Engineer | EDC platform operations | DGO staff | 1 |

## Chief Data Officer (CDO)

The CDO chairs the Data Governance Council and holds ultimate executive accountability for the governance program. The CDO does not interact with the EDC on a day-to-day basis but is responsible for:

- Approving Tier 1 Enterprise Standards.
- Resolving escalated cross-domain disputes that the DGC cannot reach consensus on.
- Representing data governance priorities to the executive leadership team.
- Reviewing quarterly governance scorecards produced by the DGO.

## Functional Domain Manager (FDM)

The FDM is the most critical operational role in the governance model. Each FDM is accountable for the completeness, accuracy, and timeliness of metadata within their Functional Domain. Specifically, the FDM is responsible for:

- Ensuring that all Data Assets within the domain are registered in the catalog and that registrations are complete (no missing required attributes).
- Assigning an Owner and, where appropriate, a Steward to every Data Asset in the domain.
- Reviewing and approving new asset registrations and classification decisions within the domain.
- Conducting or delegating quarterly data quality reviews for all Published assets (see [Conducting Data Quality Reviews](../sops/conducting-data-reviews.md)).
- Authoring and maintaining Tier 2 Domain Policies.
- Reassigning ownership when personnel changes occur (e.g., an Owner leaves the organization or transfers to a different team).
- Escalating unresolved data issues to the DGO or the DGC as appropriate.

!!! warning "FDM vacancies"
    When an FDM position is vacant — due to departure, leave, or reorganization — the CDO must designate an interim FDM within five business days. A domain cannot operate without an FDM, as new asset registrations and classification approvals require FDM sign-off.

## System Owner

A System Owner is a technical lead responsible for the metadata accuracy of Data Assets that originate from a specific source system. While the FDM governs the business context, the System Owner governs the technical context. Responsibilities include:

- Validating that schema metadata imported by connectors accurately reflects the source system's current state.
- Reviewing and resolving schema drift alerts generated by the integration layer (see [Integrations — Connector Lifecycle](../architecture/integrations.md)).
- Providing technical descriptions for columns and tables that supplement the business descriptions maintained by Stewards.
- Coordinating with the Catalog Platform Engineer when connector configuration changes are needed (e.g., adding new schemas to a crawl scope).
- Confirming that Lineage Links accurately represent the data flows into and out of their system.

A single individual may serve as System Owner for multiple systems if those systems are closely related and the workload is manageable.

## Data Steward

A Data Steward is responsible for the day-to-day quality of metadata for one or more Data Assets. Stewardship is an operational role — the Steward does the hands-on work of keeping catalog records accurate and current. Responsibilities include:

- Writing and maintaining clear, accurate descriptions for their assigned Data Assets and columns.
- Reviewing their assets during quarterly data quality reviews and documenting findings.
- Responding to questions from Data Consumers about the meaning, quality, and appropriate use of their assigned assets.
- Logging data quality issues through the issue resolution process (see [Resolving Data Issues](../sops/resolving-data-issues.md)).
- Updating asset metadata when business definitions change or when the underlying data structure evolves.

Stewardship is typically assigned to the individual closest to the data — the analyst who builds reports from it, the engineer who maintains the pipeline that produces it, or the business user who defines its meaning.

## Data Consumer

Every Meridian employee has implicit Data Consumer access to the EDC. Data Consumers can:

- Browse and search the catalog to discover Data Assets.
- View asset metadata including descriptions, classifications, ownership, and lineage.
- Request access to underlying data through the [access request process](../sops/requesting-access.md).
- Flag potential metadata issues by submitting a data issue report.

Data Consumers cannot modify catalog metadata, approve workflows, or change classifications.

## Catalog Platform Engineer

The Catalog Platform Engineer (a DGO staff member) is responsible for the technical operation of the EDC platform itself. This role does not make governance decisions but ensures the platform is available, performant, and correctly configured. Responsibilities include:

- Monitoring connector health and resolving ingestion failures.
- Managing the EDC's deployment pipeline and coordinating releases across DEV, STAGING, and PROD.
- Administering API client registrations and rate limit tier assignments.
- Performing database maintenance, backup verification, and capacity planning.
- Implementing platform configuration changes requested by FDMs or the DGO (e.g., adding new connector instances, modifying workflow rules).

## RACI Matrix

The following matrix summarizes responsibility assignments for key governance activities:

| Activity | CDO | FDM | System Owner | Steward | Consumer | Platform Eng. |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Register new Data Asset | — | **A** | C | **R** | — | — |
| Assign classification | — | **A** | C | **R** | — | — |
| Conduct quarterly review | I | **A** | C | **R** | — | — |
| Resolve data issue | — | **A** | C | **R** | I | — |
| Approve access request | — | **A** | — | C | **R** | — |
| Approve Tier 1 policy | **A** | C | I | I | — | — |
| Manage connector config | — | I | **R** | — | — | **A** |
| Platform release mgmt | — | — | — | — | — | **R/A** |

**R** = Responsible (does the work), **A** = Accountable (approves/owns the outcome), **C** = Consulted, **I** = Informed.
